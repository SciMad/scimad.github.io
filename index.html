MIT Technology and Innovation Bootcamp, Tokyo (2019)

What is MIT Bootcamp?

Accelerated Learnning

MIT Bootcamp is an Accelerated-Learning Program by MIT to learn and practice innovation and leadership principles taught at MIT. The webinars, video lectures, other materials and the bootcamp cover "one semester course of MIT Sloan School of Management"

Lectures & Sessions

The participants get trainings, lectures from scientists, MIT faculty and MIT-trained mentors on Robotics, AI and IoT on innovation, technology and entrepreneurship. Bootcampers learn the same innovation framework and curriculum taught in core entrepreneurship classes at MIT, and practice the critical skills required to be leaders and drive innovation and collaboration.

Developing and Sustaining an Innovation

Innovators and entrepreneurs from all over the world will work on project teams to start a new venture in one week. The Bootcamp will provide with experiences innovators face in the first 1-2 years of a new venture.

Interaction with Global Commmunity

The Bootcamps attract a diverse group of innovators from around the world. Creating an innovation and starting a company can be greatly simplified with a group of global innovators and entrepreneurs

Who are the participants?

Past Bootcampers have come from a diversity of backgrounds. Innovators, designers, early stage and seriall enterpreneurs, graduate students and researchers, business owners, leaders, doctors, educators and more diverse community come together at the Bootcamp.
